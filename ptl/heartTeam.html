Bible stories remind us why we should be humble and we should recognize that all of us are vulnerable to our sinful nature and we should at all time guard our hearts against evil thoughts. Bible teaches us not to be arrogant or complacent, so we can stand firm against our sinful nature (tendency), like jealousy of others' success and hatred against other people. Value those who care enough about you to show you your errors and risk your friendship. It is better to tell people what they need to hear than to tell them what they want to hear.

When the problem seems to be too big, just remember that God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob is bigger. So, don't lose heart. Be strong and have faith. Trust in him, seek him and put him first. That means to learn the bible and to put the teaching into practice. Everything else will then fall in place. We are not god ourselves nor should we try to be god as a misguided high school student suggested in an open mic event while holding his crotch. There is only one God, the creator of the universe and the one who saves people from a rut of indulging in sins.

不給魔鬼留地步 I admire Shadrach, Meshach and Abednego because they give no ground to Satan. They do not go around and complain why God is not doing this or that for them. When the King of Babylon Nebuchadnezzar threatened to burn them alive if they would not bow down to worship the gold statue of the king. The three friends of Daniel would not give any ground to Satan. Whether their God will save them or not, they refused to compromise on their principle of justice and fairness. They would not go back on their contract with the God of Abraham, Isaac and Jacob. They were determined not to bow down to any other gods or worship them.
