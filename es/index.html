Why learn about Physics?

Before we delve into the nitty, gritty details in Physics, let us explore some of the reasons for studying physics. As the saying goes: If there is a why, there will be a how. Once you know why you are studying physics, then you will have reasons to presevere to learn physics even in the face of difficulties in your study of physics.
  • Physics teaches us to think for ourselves and check out statements instead of just blindly accepting claims of truth.
    ex: Is it true that a narrow paper airplane flies farther than a wider paper airplane?
  • Physics enables us to make prediction.
    ex: Based on previous data and conclusions, can we determine at what angle above the ground a basketball should be thrown so it wooshes ito the basket?
  • Also, physics is the foundation for engineering and sciences.
    ex: If a person wants to invent a useful tool, there are usually some physics that the person will use to design and build the tool.
Physics helps us become more self-reliant, confident and fulfilled. Also, we will be gaining a sense of accomplishment when we apply what we learn in physcis to different areas of our lives. Enjoy your study of physics!
