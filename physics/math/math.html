Key Things to Know in Math

  • The angle is always measured from the normal line in waves questions.
  • All quantities have untis, except coefficient of friction and index of refraction.
  • To use the physics formuala, the unit for mass must be measured in kilogram, the unit for length must be measured in meters, the unit for time must be measured in seconds, the unit for work or energy must be measured in joules, the unit for power must be in watt.
  • The prefixes are power of ten multiplied to the unit.

Key Words in Math

direct relationship, linearly proportional relationship, inverse (indirect) relationship, magnitude

  • Direct relationship: when one variable increases, the other variable also increases.
  • Inverse relationship: When one variable increases, the other variable decreases.
  • Linear relationship: The ratio of one variable to the other variable is constant.
  • magnitude: the absolute value of a quantity.

Formula in Math

  • Area of a rectangle A = bh
  • Area of a circle = π r2
  • Circumferebce C = π r2
  • c2 = a2 + b2
  • sin (θ) = a c
  • cos (θ) = b c
  • tan (θ) = a b

Example 1

How many amperes (A) are in 130 milliamperes (mA)?

electrical current I = 130 mA × (10-3 A / 1 mA) = 130 × 10-3 A = 0.130 A

Example 2

Q: How do you convert 23 grams to kilograms?
m = 23 g × (1 kg / 103 g) = 0.023 kg because k = 1 kilo = 103

Example 4

A cart travels 4.00 meters east and then 3.00 meters north. Determine the magnitude of the cart’s resultant displacement

Draw the vectors head to tail and draw the resultant from tail of first vector to the head of the second vector. c2 = a2 + b2; b = 4 m, a = 3m,
c = √(a2 + b2) = √((3 m)2 + (4 m)2) = 5 m
