Key Things to Know in Waves

  • Sound waves are longitudinal and mechanical.
  • Light wave are transverse and electromagnetic.
  • Transverse wave particles vibrate side to side perpendicular to the wave direction.
  • Longitudinal wave particles vibrate back and forth parallel to the wave direction.
  • Light can be polarized, but longitudinal waves like sound wave cannot be polarized
  • The amplitude of a sound wave determines its energy.
  • The frequency of an electromagnetic wave determines its energy.
  • Refraction is the bending of a wave as the wave enters a different medium (substance).
  • Diffraction is the spreading of a wave as the wave passes through an opening.
  • As a wave travels from one substance into another substance, the wave's frequency remains the same.
  • All electromagnetic waves travel at the speed of light c in vacuum. c = 3 × 108 m/s
  • Doppler Effect refers to an observer perceiving an observed frequency that is different from the actual frequency of the wave. When a wave source approaches an observer, the observer perceives a higher frequency than the frequency of the wave. when a wave source recedes (moving away) from an observer, the observer perceives a lower frequency than the frequency of the wave.
  • All angles are measured from the normal.
  • Two points on a wave are in phase if they are separated by 360° or integer multiple of 360°.
  • Two points on a wave are 180° out of phase if they are separated by 180° or 180° + 360° n, where n = integer.

Key Words in Modern Physics

antinode, frequency, amplitude, period, wavelength, pitch, loudness, wavelength, standing wave, resonance, natural frequency

  • Frequency: the number of waves passing by a point per second.
  • Wavelength: the length of a wave.
  • Period: the time for one complete vibration to happen
  • Amplitude: the height of a wave.
  • Node: a stationary point on a standing wave.
  • Antinode: a most displaced point on a standing wave.

Example 1

a) Which characteristic of a light wave must remain the same as the light wave passes from glass into air?
(1) amplitude (3) period
(2) frequency (4) wavelength

Frequency of a wave remains the same as the wave travels from one substance into another substance.


b) Which characteristic of a light wave must increase as the light wave passes from glass into air?
(1) amplitude (3) period
(2) frequency (4) wavelength

Glass has a higher index of refraction n. Air has a lowest index of refraction. This can be seen from p.2 of the physics reference table. v = c n . Since air has a smaller n, therefore the speed of light in air vair is greater than vglass. The speed must be greater for light in air. Since v = f λ, and f remains the same as the light enters into air from glass, the wavelength λ of the light wave must be greater in air.

Example 2

A duck floating on a lake oscillates up and down 5.0 times during a 10.-second interval as a periodic wave passes by. What is the frequency of the duck’s oscillations?

Frequency of a wave = # of waves per second. = (# of waves) ÷ (time) = 5 waves ÷ 10 s = 0.5 Hz;
A wave = an oscillation up and down.
