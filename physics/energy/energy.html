Energy

One exciting thing from energy is the gliding through the air on a mountain bike or in a wingsuit.



Quantities in Energy

Name Symbol Unit Type
Total energy ET, ETOT J, joules scalar
Potential energy, gravitational potential energy PE J, joules scalar
Kinetic energy KE J, joules scalar
Internal energy, Heat energy Q J, Joules scalar
Work W J, joules scalar
Power P W, watts scalar
Force F N, newtons vector

Key Things to Know in Energy

  • Total Energy of an object is sum of all the energy possessed by the object.
  • Mechanical Energy = Potential Energy + Kinetic Energy
  • Work, potential energy PE, kinetic energy KE are measured in the unit of Joule J = kg · m2/sec2
  • An eV is an energy unit equal to 1.6 × 10-19 joules.
  • Gravitational potential energy increases as height increases.
  • Kinetic energy changes only if velocity changes.
  • When air friction is negligible or may be ignored, the mechanical energy of an object (PE + KE) remains constant as the object is displaced to different position.
  • When work is done on an object, the object's initial energy is increased to its final energy by the amount of work done on it.
  • Power is the rate of change of work.

Key Words in Energy

Energy, Mechanical energy, Elastic potential energy, Gravitational energy, kinetic energy, power, work

  • Energy: the ability of an object to do work.
  • Work: work is done when a force moves an object.
  • Elastic potential energy: energy stored in a spring.
  • Gravitational potential energy: energy stored in an object due to its height position.
  • kinetic energy: the energy of an object because of its motion.
  • Power: the rate at which work is done.

Formula in Energy

  • KE = 1 2 mv2
  • PE = mgh
  • PEs = 1 2 k x2
  • E = PE + KE + Q
  • W = Fd
  • Ei + W = Ef
  • P = W t

Example 1

A block slides across a rough, horizontal tabletop. As the block comes to rest, there is an increase in the block-tabletop system’s
(1) gravitational potential energy
(2) elastic potential energy
(3) kinetic energy
(4) internal (thermal) energy

h = fixed, PE = mgh = fixed. v = smaller, KE = decrease. Mechanical energy decreases, but the total energy must remain the same. Therefore, the energy is transfered into the heat energy account. The heat energy (internal energy) is increased.

Example 2

A pendulum is made from a 7.50-kilogram mass attached to a rope connected to the ceiling of a gymnasium. The mass is pushed to the side until it is at position A, 1.5 meters higher than its equilibrium position. After it is released from rest at position A, the pendulum moves freely back and forth between positions A and B, as shown in the diagram below.

What is the total amount of kinetic energy that the mass has as it swings freely through its equilibrium position? [Neglect friction.]
(1) 11 J (3) 110 J
(2) 94 J (4) 920 J

E = PE + KE = remains fixed. At position A, EA = PE + KE = mgh + 0 = 7.5 kg × 9.8 m/s2 × 1.5 m = 110.25 J. EB = EA. EB = mgh + KE = 0 + KE = KE. KEB = 110.25 J
